What Is FRP Lock?

What is FRP?

Factory Reset Protection (FRP) is a security feature found on many Android devices. It helps protect your phone if it is lost or stolen by asking for the Google account that was previously used on the device after a factory reset.

When does FRP appear?

FRP may appear after:

  • A factory reset.
  • Resetting the device without first removing the Google account.
  • Buying a used phone that still has the previous owner's account.

How can you avoid FRP?

Before resetting your phone:

  • Remove your Google account from the device.
  • Make sure you know your Google account email and password.

How to Find Your Phone's IMEI Number

The IMEI (International Mobile Equipment Identity) is a unique number that identifies your phone.

You can usually find it by:

  • Dialing *#06#.
  • Looking in Settings → About Phone (location varies by manufacturer).
  • Checking the SIM tray or the label on the device (on some models).
  • Looking on the original phone box.

When requesting services that require the IMEI, always double-check that you provide the correct number.

What Is a Network Unlock?

A network unlock allows a compatible phone to accept SIM cards from different mobile carriers instead of being restricted to one carrier.

Common reasons to unlock a phone include:

  • Switching to another mobile network.
  • Using a local SIM card while traveling.
  • Purchasing a carrier-locked device and becoming eligible for unlocking.

Availability depends on the phone model, carrier, and eligibility requirements.

What Is Phone Flashing?

Phone flashing is the process of installing or reinstalling a device's operating system or firmware.

It may be used to:

  • Repair software problems.
  • Restore a phone after software corruption.
  • Install an official firmware version.

Because flashing can erase data or affect device functionality if done incorrectly, it should be performed carefully and with the correct firmware.

How to Check Your Phone Model

Knowing your exact phone model helps ensure you request the correct service.

You can usually find it:

  • In Settings → About Phone.
  • On the original packaging.
  • On the back of some devices.
  • Under the battery on certain older phones.

Before You Request an Unlock

Before submitting an unlock request:

  • Confirm your phone brand and model.
  • Provide accurate contact information.
  • If requested, provide the correct IMEI number.
  • Describe the issue clearly.

Accurate information helps reduce delays.
